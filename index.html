‚ú® What is Teeth Whitening?

Teeth whitening is a cosmetic dental treatment designed to remove stains and discoloration from teeth. Over time, teeth can become stained from coffee, tea, tobacco, certain foods, and natural aging. Whitening procedures lighten the teeth and help restore a bright, healthy smile.

The process can be done either professionally by a dentist, which typically uses higher-concentration bleaching agents, or with over-the-counter products like whitening strips, toothpaste, and trays.

ü¶∑ Common Causes of Tooth Discoloration

Understanding the causes of tooth discoloration can help you prevent stains and maintain your results after whitening. Common factors include:

  • Food & Drinks: Coffee, tea, red wine, cola, and certain berries.
  • Smoking & Tobacco: Both cigarettes and chewing tobacco lead to yellowing and stains.
  • Aging: Over time, enamel wears down, revealing the yellowish dentin beneath.
  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Inadequate brushing and flossing can lead to plaque buildup and staining.
  • Medications: Certain antibiotics (like tetracycline) and antihistamines can cause intrinsic staining.

ü¶∑ Is teeth whitening safe?
Yes, when performed under professional supervision or using dentist-approved products, teeth whitening is considered very safe. Some sensitivity may occur but is typically temporary.
‚è≥ How long do the results last?
Results can last anywhere from 6 months to 2 years, depending on your habits. Avoiding staining foods, practicing good oral hygiene, and occasional touch-ups can extend results.
üò¨ Will it cause tooth sensitivity?
Some individuals may experience temporary sensitivity during or after whitening. This usually subsides within a few days. Using sensitive toothpaste can help.
üè† Can I do it at home or should I visit a dentist?
Both options are valid. Professional whitening offers faster, more effective results, while home kits are more affordable but take longer. For best safety and results, consult your dentist.
