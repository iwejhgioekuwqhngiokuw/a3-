Technology and Architecture at the Service of Dentistry

Our goal is to show how the main technological innovations can transform the daily routine of a dental office. In addition, we explore how to apply architectural principles to create functional, modern spaces that are prepared to integrate these technologies in an intelligent and efficient way.

Intraoral Scanners

Intraoral scanners create accurate 3D images of the mouth using LED light, replacing traditional messy molds. They speed up the production of crowns, aligners, and other dental devices, offering more comfort and fewer appointments.

Source: https://dentalsorrisoslz.com.br/o-que-e-o-scanner-intraoral/

3D Printing

3D printers turn digital dental models into physical items like crowns, aligners, dentures, and surgical guides. This technology allows clinics to produce devices in-house quickly, often the same day.

Source: blog.suryadental.com.br/impressora-3d-odontologia

Management Software

Dental management software streamlines scheduling, patient records, billing, and inventory. It boosts efficiency, reduces errors, and improves patient communication with automated reminders and online access.

Source: https://www.codental.com.br/

Digital Radiography

Digital X-rays use sensors instead of film, producing faster, clearer images with less radiation. Portable devices allow dentists to take images right at the treatment chair for quicker diagnoses.

Source: https://blog.dentalspeed.com/raio-x-portatil-odontologico-e-sensor-digital/

Needle-Free Electronic Anesthesia

This method uses pressurized jets to deliver anesthesia without needles, making treatments nearly painless. It reduces patient anxiety, provides precise numbing, and speeds up recovery.

Source: https://clinicaorbemdalmolin.com.br/especialidade/anestesia-sem- agulha

Intraoral Scanners Setup

Applied principles: Functionality, ergonomics, and operational flow.

  • Location: Close to the dental chair, with mobile or articulated support for easy handling.
  • Ergonomics: Within the dentistâ€™s reach to avoid unnecessary movement.
  • Infrastructure: Needs power outlets and USB/network connections for integration with the clinic's system.

3D Printers Setup

Applied principles: Functionality, safety, and environmental control.

  • Dedicated space: Install in a separate room or ventilated area due to particle and vapor emissions.
  • Sound insulation: Consider soundproofing if placed near clinical areas since some printers are noisy.
  • Workflow: Must integrate seamlessly with the digital workflow from the intraoral scanner.

Management Software Integration

Applied principles: Organization, functionality, and comfort.

  • Digital integration: Connect workstations at reception and treatment rooms to the same network.
  • Accessibility: Use tablets or touchscreens for more agile operations.
  • Clean design: Maintain organized digital interfaces to reduce clutter in physical spaces.

Digital Radiography Design

Applied principles: Safety, efficiency, and mobility.

  • Shielding: Fixed X-ray rooms must meet radiation protection standards (lead walls, warning signs).
  • Portable equipment: Should be safely stored and easily charged for mobility inside treatment rooms.
  • Lighting: Use indirect lighting to improve image visibility on monitors.
